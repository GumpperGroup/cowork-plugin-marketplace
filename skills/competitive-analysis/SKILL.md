# Competitive Bio Analysis

Understanding how competing agents present themselves online reveals positioning opportunities and helps identify differentiation strategies that AI search engines will reward.

## When to Perform Competitive Analysis

- Before writing a new bio (to establish differentiation from the start)
- When the agent feels they're not being recommended by AI tools
- When the agent names specific competitors they want to differentiate from
- When entering a new market or specialty area

## Analysis Framework

### Step 1: Identify Competitors

Ask the agent to name 3–5 agents they consider direct competitors, or search for:
- Top agents in the same ZIP code/city on Zillow
- Agents with the most reviews in their target area
- Agents who appear when you ask AI tools "best REALTOR® in [City]"

### Step 2: Audit Each Competitor

For each competitor, evaluate:

**Online Presence:**
- Which platforms have their bio?
- Do they have a personal website with an About page?
- Is there schema.org markup on their page?
- How many reviews on Google, Zillow, and Realtor.com?

**Bio Content Quality:**
- Do they use specific stats (volume, transactions, years)?
- Do they have designations listed and spelled out?
- Do they use natural language query phrasing?
- Is the bio entity-rich or marketing-fluffy?

**EEAT Signals:**
- Score their Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust signals
- Identify which EEAT areas they're weakest in

**Positioning:**
- What specialties do they claim?
- What neighborhoods/areas do they focus on?
- What buyer/seller type do they target?
- What is their unique differentiator (if any)?

### Step 3: Identify Opportunities

Look for:
1. **Underserved niches**: Specialties no competitor claims (e.g., VA loans, historic homes, 55+ communities, new construction)
2. **Credential gaps**: Designations your agent has that competitors don't mention
3. **Review advantages**: If your agent has more/better reviews, emphasize this
4. **Geographic white space**: Neighborhoods competitors don't name specifically
5. **Content gaps**: Types of proof points competitors aren't providing
6. **Technical gaps**: Competitors without schema markup or structured bios

### Step 4: Position the Agent

Based on the analysis, craft positioning that:
- Explicitly claims the niches competitors leave open
- Leads with the EEAT signals competitors lack
- Uses the query phrasing competitors haven't adopted
- Establishes a clear, defensible differentiator

## Ethical Guidelines

- Focus on analyzing public information only
- Never disparage competitors in bio copy
- Differentiate through strength, not by attacking weakness
- If competitors have strengths, acknowledge them privately and find a different angle
- Never claim to be "#1" unless verifiable through a named ranking source
