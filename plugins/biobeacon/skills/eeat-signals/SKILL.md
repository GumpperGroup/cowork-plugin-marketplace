# EEAT Signal Optimization for Real Estate Agents

EEAT (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) is Google's framework for evaluating content quality. AI search engines use similar signals when deciding which agents to recommend. This skill helps you systematically strengthen all four pillars.

## Experience Signals

Experience means demonstrating firsthand, real-world involvement. For real estate agents, this includes:

**Must-Have Signals:**
- Years actively licensed (not just "experienced" — state the number)
- Total transaction count or annual transaction count
- Total or annual sales volume in dollars
- Types of transactions handled (buyers, sellers, both, commercial, residential)
- Geographic breadth (single city, multi-county, multi-state)

**Strong Differentiators:**
- Personal experience with the process (e.g., "As someone who has relocated three times, I understand...")
- Specific market events navigated (e.g., "Guided 12 clients through the 2020 market shift")
- Before/after market expertise (e.g., "Active in the Raleigh market since before the tech boom")

**How to Write It:**
```
Weak: "Experienced agent with years in the business"
Strong: "Since earning her North Carolina real estate license in 2012, Sarah has closed over 340 residential transactions totaling more than $128 million in sales volume across Wake and Durham counties."
```

## Expertise Signals

Expertise means specialized knowledge and qualifications beyond basic licensure.

**Must-Have Signals:**
- Professional designations with full names: CRS, ABR, SRS, GRI, SRES, etc.
- Relevant certifications (e.g., Certified Negotiation Expert, Military Relocation Professional)
- Education credentials if relevant (degrees in finance, law, architecture, etc.)
- Continuing education or specialized training

**Strong Differentiators:**
- Published articles, market reports, or educational content
- Speaking engagements at industry events
- Teaching or mentoring roles
- Niche expertise (luxury, new construction, historic homes, investment properties, 1031 exchanges)

**Designation Reference:**
| Acronym | Full Name | Signals |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| CRS | Certified Residential Specialist | Top 3% of REALTORS® |
| ABR | Accredited Buyer's Representative | Buyer advocacy expertise |
| SRS | Seller Representative Specialist | Listing/seller expertise |
| GRI | Graduate, REALTOR® Institute | Advanced real estate education |
| SRES | Seniors Real Estate Specialist | 55+ market expertise |
| CLHMS | Certified Luxury Home Marketing Specialist | Luxury market expertise |
| MRP | Military Relocation Professional | Military/VA expertise |
| PSA | Pricing Strategy Advisor | CMA and pricing expertise |
| RENE | Real Estate Negotiation Expert | Negotiation specialization |
| e-PRO | NAR e-PRO Certification | Digital marketing expertise |

## Authoritativeness Signals

Authoritativeness means being recognized by others as a leading voice or authority.

**Must-Have Signals:**
- Awards with specifics (awarding body, year, ranking)
- Association memberships and leadership roles
- MLS board involvement
- Brokerage recognition (top producer tier, chairman's circle, etc.)

**Strong Differentiators:**
- Media appearances or quotes (local news, industry publications)
- Industry leadership (board positions, committee chairs)
- Community involvement with named organizations
- Testimonials from other industry professionals

**How to Write It:**
```
Weak: "Award-winning agent recognized by her peers"
Strong: "Recognized as a Triangle Business Journal Top 25 Residential Agent in 2023 and 2024, and serves as the 2024 Chair of the Raleigh Regional Association of REALTORS® Technology Committee."
```

## Trustworthiness Signals

Trust is the most important EEAT factor for YMYL (Your Money, Your Life) topics — and real estate qualifies.

**Must-Have Signals:**
- Verifiable review scores with counts (e.g., "4.9 stars across 142 reviews on Google and Zillow")
- Named brokerage affiliation with physical office address
- License number and state (or a link to license verification)
- Clear contact information
- No history of disciplinary actions (don't mention; just ensure there aren't any)

**Strong Differentiators:**
- Client testimonial excerpts attributed to named (or initialed) clients
- Professional liability insurance / E&O coverage mention
- Fair housing commitment statement
- Better Business Bureau rating
- Realtor.com or Zillow "verified" status

**How to Write It:**
```
Weak: "Trusted by hundreds of happy clients"
Strong: "Maintains a 4.9-star rating across 156 verified client reviews on Zillow and Google Business, with a 98% client satisfaction rate tracked through post-closing surveys."
```

## EEAT Audit Checklist

When auditing a bio, score each signal as present (✅), missing (❌), or weak (⚠️):

### Experience
- [ ] Years licensed (specific number)
- [ ] Transaction count
- [ ] Sales volume (dollar amount)
- [ ] Transaction types
- [ ] Geographic scope

### Expertise
- [ ] Professional designations (spelled out)
- [ ] Certifications
- [ ] Education (if relevant)
- [ ] Niche specializations

### Authoritativeness
- [ ] Awards (with year and awarding body)
- [ ] Association memberships/roles
- [ ] Media mentions
- [ ] Industry recognition

### Trustworthiness
- [ ] Review scores with counts
- [ ] Brokerage name and affiliation
- [ ] License information
- [ ] Contact information
- [ ] Fair housing statement

Present this checklist to the agent when identifying gaps in their bio.
